# Week 2 Quiz: Embodied Intelligence and Applications

## Question 1: Multiple Choice
What is the core principle of embodied intelligence?

A) Intelligence is purely computational and independent of physical form
B) Intelligence emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment
C) Intelligence requires human-like appearance
D) Intelligence is only possible with advanced sensors

## Question 2: Multiple Choice

A) The process of changing a robot's physical form
B) The idea that physical properties of the body can perform computations
C) The computation of body measurements
D) The morphing of one robot into another

## Question 4: Multiple Choice

A) Manufacturing and Assembly

## Question 5: Short Answer
Explain the concept of "affordances" in the context of embodied intelligence and give an example.

## Question 6: Multiple Choice
What does the term "embodiment hypothesis" suggest?

A) That robots must look like humans to be intelligent
B) That the physical form and properties of an agent shape its cognitive processes
C) That robots must be embodied in virtual reality to learn
D) That embodiment is just a design aesthetic choice

## Question 7: Multiple Choice
Which of the following is an example of environmental scaffolding in embodied intelligence?

A) A robot with very high computational power
B) A robot that uses the environment as a form of external memory
C) A robot that operates in a completely isolated environment
D) A robot with many sensors but no actuators

## Question 8: Short Answer
Describe one challenge and one benefit of using humanoid robots in healthcare applications.

## Question 9: Multiple Choice
What is "tight sensorimotor coupling" in embodied intelligence?

A) The separation of sensing and motor functions
B) The importance of real-time coupling between perception and action
C) The use of only sensors without motors
D) The tight physical connection between robot parts

## Question 10: Multiple Choice
Which application area involves robots that work alongside humans, combining human dexterity with robotic precision?

A) Surgical Robotics
B) Collaborative Robots (Cobots)
C) Autonomous Mobile Robots
D) Entertainment Robots

---

## Answer Key

1. B) Intelligence emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment
2. B) The idea that physical properties of the body can perform computations
3. A) True
4. C) Purely Digital Computing
5. Affordances describe action possibilities that are directly perceivable from the environment. For example, a handle affords grasping, and a surface affords support.
6. B) That the physical form and properties of an agent shape its cognitive processes
7. B) A robot that uses the environment as a form of external memory
8. Challenge: Ensuring safety and regulatory approval for medical applications. Benefit: Providing consistent, precise assistance with daily activities for patients.
9. B) The importance of real-time coupling between perception and action
10. B) Collaborative Robots (Cobots)
